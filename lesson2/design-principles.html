Design Principles Document

Kylar Sorensen

Proximity

Religious Studies Center

https://rsc.byu.edu/

In the Religious Studies Center website each individual article or story is linked closely together. They are separated by white space that is a different color than the cards that link to each article. A picture, the date it was published, and the first few lines of the article are included in each card, followed by white space on all sides. The white space is of a different color which makes it easier to distinguish. It also makes each article just a little further apart, making it obvious that each contains a different topic, idea, element, etc.

Contrast

Bankrate

https://www.bankrate.com/

While the Bankrate page may not be super colorful or vibrant, it is very professional and legible. All the information is clear and easy to read because there is great contrast between the colors. The primary two colors are black and white, and it is tough to get any more contrast than what is found between those two colors. The third color that appears throughout the site is a darker blue, which contrasts well with white, but not black. Hence, there are not very many spots throughout the site where black can be found close to blue.

Alignment

Smithsonian

https://www.si.edu/

Throughout the Smithsonian website one can find examples of great use of alignment, but especially towards the bottom of the homepage were everything is centered over one another. The pictures, headings and short descriptions are all stacked one above the other and centered. Just above this section the pictures are still centered; however, the headings and the text are all justified to the left. This along with the color change makes it obvious that the section is changing and, with it, the content and focus as well.
